Customer Story
Voltalia: from regional fragmentation to a single source of truth across 16 countries
The challenge
Voltalia's project teams operated across 16 countries with data living in Excel files, email chains, and paper — each handoff risked losing historical context. Regional standardization was nearly impossible without a common data layer.
What they built
They centralized all project data into a single Airtable base with customizable interfaces for each regional team and automated workflows for cross-region reporting. Every site sees the same live data, not a lagged export.
Why it matters for Paradigm
Paradigm coordinates trials across 166 provider organizations — each with its own systems, timelines, and reporting cadences. The same fragmentation that cost Voltalia visibility across 16 countries is exactly what SPIRE study portfolios face across academic centers and community oncology practices. A single source of truth eliminates the manual reconciliation cycle before every sponsor update.

Customer Story
Raising Cane's: standardized, mobile-first operations across 1,000+ locations
The challenge
Opening new restaurant locations required coordinating dozens of teams — facilities, finance, training, marketing — across a 200-day timeline with no shared system. Status lived in spreadsheets and inboxes, making portfolio-level visibility impossible.
What they built
They created structured intake workflows, timeline-driven coordination architecture, and mobile-first interfaces for field teams — all feeding a centralized operations hub leadership could check in real time.
Why it matters for Paradigm
Paradigm's SPIRE study launches require the same kind of coordinated ramp: site selection, protocol readiness, patient identification, data setup, and sponsor alignment — all running concurrently across site types that operate very differently from each other. A structured, timeline-driven coordination layer gives ops teams the same portfolio visibility Raising Cane's now takes for granted.
