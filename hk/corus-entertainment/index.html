Customer Story
A diversified media group turns disconnected tools into 22 million pounds of impact
The problem
A large European media group with television, radio, publishing, and digital brands had subscription sales spread across disjointed tools. That fragmentation capped how many revenue experiments the team could run and how quickly they could learn from them.
What changed
They stood up Airtable in a single afternoon. 200 people moved onto one connected place to manage, document, and report on experiments across every advertising and commercial channel, without a long implementation or a rip-and-replace.
Why it matters for Corus
Corus runs a comparable mix of networks, radio, and digital brands under real revenue pressure. A connected operating layer lets a leaner team run more revenue experiments and see results faster, which is exactly the leverage a turnaround needs.

One source of truth for global content
The problem
Red Bull Media House produces time-sensitive content across dozens of channels, in over 170 countries and 36 languages. Every team worked in its own silo, with no central place to plan, produce, and distribute.
What changed
They built one source of truth, the Media Planet Hub, a global creative production pipeline that plans and executes on every piece of content created worldwide.
Why it matters for Corus
Corus coordinates content and campaigns across networks, radio, and streaming. One connected pipeline keeps a distributed, fast-moving organization aligned without adding overhead, so output holds up even as the team gets leaner.
