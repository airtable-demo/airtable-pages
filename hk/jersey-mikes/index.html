Customer Story
Raising Cane's: new-restaurant openings and field audits
Challenge
New-restaurant openings ran on a 200-day preparation timeline from excavation to open day, while quarterly assessments sent about 50 assessors to roughly 1,000 restaurants. Both lived in Excel and Outlook, with facilities, security, and finance stakeholders working from separate versions.
Solution
A connected, timeline-driven system now runs openings and field audits together. Assessors self-schedule against rule checks like the 28-day wait between visits, and a real-time availability dashboard keeps every stakeholder on the same readiness picture.
Result
Openings and audits run on one source of truth spanning restaurant operations, quality assurance, training, marketing, facilities, security, and finance, instead of a patchwork of spreadsheets.
Why it matters for Jersey Mike's
A build from roughly 3,300 to about 15,000 locations turns openings and field audits into a repeatable, visible system a lean corporate team can run at IPO scale.

Customer Story
Walmart and Sam's Club: retail operations at scale
Challenge
Walmart and Sam's Club planned and executed product portfolio work across more than five business pillars, with strategy and status fragmented across Excel, Jira, and Confluence.
Solution
One connected view links strategic pillars, problems, and initiatives, centralizes annual-operating-plan alignment, and tracks dependencies. It syncs bidirectionally with Jira and connects to Confluence and GitHub, working alongside those systems rather than replacing them.
Result
Teams across product management, engineering, program management, leadership, and finance work from one source of truth for strategy and execution.
Why it matters for Jersey Mike's
Public-market scale rewards one trusted view of the plan that connects to the tools each team already uses, from the point-of-sale to project trackers, without a rip and replace.
